Visual VRDC Overview

What is the VRDC?

  • The Virtual Research Data Center (VRDC) is an analytic environment that enables researchers to access and analyze CMS program data stored in the Chronic Conditions Warehouse (CCW).
  • CMS Chronic Conditions Data Warehouse (CCW) provides researchers with Medicare and Medicaid beneficiary, claims, and assessment data linked by beneficiary across the continuum of care.
  • Researchers connect remotely to the VRDC from their own workstations to perform data analyses.
  • Benefits of using VRDC include:
    • Secure environment to analyze beneficiary level data.
    • No Data management plan required.
    • Dedicated workspace only accessible to the study team.

How do you access it?

  • Access to the CCW VRDC is granted to researchers with an approved Data Use Agreement (DUA) from CMS.
    • Under the approved DUA, individual users are given access with a 'seat', which can only be allocated to 1 individual, and each 'seat' has an associated fee.
  • VRDC access requires obtaining a CCW user ID, which typically takes three to four business days, depending on user completion of specific tasks including:
    • Completing identity proofing through Experian
    • Annual Security Awareness Training (SAT)
    • Setting up multi-factor authentication (MFA)
    • Downloading VMware Horizon Client
    • Connecting to the VRDC virtual desktop
  • Once connected, users log in to SAS® Enterprise Guide®; library access is usually granted within two to three business days after CMS confirms the approved DUA and payment.
  • For information on requesting access, visit the "About the VRDC and Requesting Access" section on the CMS website or explore the Research Data Assistance Center (ResDAC) website at www.resdac.org.

What are the requirements to use?

  • Must be physically present in the U.S. or U.S. territories and connect from a registered U.S. IP address. (Do not attempt to access VRDC outside of the U.S.)
  • Meet CCW security requirements, including annual security training and HIPAA compliance.
  • Comply with CCW data output review process to protect the confidentiality of Medicare and Medicaid beneficiaries and adhere to CMS-approved access guidelines.
  • Do not disclose confidential information such as Protected Health Information (PHI), Personally Identifiable Information (PII), and small cell sizes (<11).
  • The CCW VRDC supports Windows but does not support Mac.
  • Systems must be updated to meet CMS standards and install the latest Virtual Desktop Infrastructure (VDI) client.
    • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required via Okta enrollment.
    • Click here to access CCW Okta Factor Enrollment and Management Guide.
Our other recommendations are to use the latest version of Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, or Microsoft Edge and to review the CCW Secure File Transfer System (SFTS) User Guide.
