HIPAA Privacy

  • What are the differences between the types of available CMS data files (i.e., Research Identifiable Files, Limited Data Set Files, and Public Use Files)?
    • CMS has three different types of data files available to investigators grouped into two categories:
      • Identifiable Data Files:
        • Research Identifiable Files (RIFs) are beneficiary-level PHI files that are specifically disclosed for research purposes in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) Privacy requirements at 45 C.F.R. ยง164.512 (i) and other applicable laws.
        • Limited Data Set (LDS) Files are beneficiary-level files that contain PHI but do not contain specific direct identifiers, as set forth in the HIPAA Privacy Rule at 45 C.F.R. ยง164.514 (e)(2).
      • Non-identifiable Data Files:
        • Public Use Files (PUFs) are also known as non-identifiable data files because they do not contain PHI or PII and are fully de-identified in accordance with HIPAA Privacy Rule requirements and the CMS cell suppression policy.
      • RIFs and LDS are only available for research and require a CMS DUA.
      • PUFs do not require a DUA and can be accessed freely available online. Please visit this website to learn more about CMS Public Use Files for Research Use.
      • For additional information, visit this website.
  • How do I know which type of file I am using?
    • If a DUA is required to access the CMS data, it is identifiable data (e.g., RIF and LDS). The identifiers in your data request determine whether the data file is RIF or LDS.
    • Visit this website for a description of the CMS-identifiable data files available to researchers, including the privacy level of the data (see example below).



  • Is the use of CMS data through the Virtual Research Data Center always considered to be human subjects research?
    • All research projects requesting access to CMS identifiable data files require review by an Institutional Review Board (IRB) or privacy board to ensure compliance with the Common Rule and the HIPAA Privacy Rule.
    • The IRB will determine whether the research project is expedited, exempt, or requires full board review.
  • Does this determination depend on the files accessed or other factors?
    • Yes, CMS must ensure that all research requests that include identifiable information (e.g., RIFs and LDS files) meet the requirements under the Common Rule and the HIPAA Privacy Rule. For more information, please visit the IRB Common Rule and HIPAA Waiver Approval section on the ResDAC website.
  • Does CMS need documentation from the IRB of either a determination of exempt status or protocol review (full or expedited)?
    • Yes, investigators are required to submit the following information as part of the RIF DUA request:
      • If subject to the Common Rule, 1) documentation of IRB approval of the research AND 2) informed consent of research subjects OR IRB waiver of the requirement to obtain informed consent. If exempt from the Common Rule, a signed and dated document with the determination from your local IRB.
      • If subject to the HIPAA Privacy Rule, individual authorization of the use of the data for the research OR documentation that an IRB or a Privacy Board has approved a waiver of research subjects' authorization for use/disclosure of information about them for research purposes.
    • For additional information on the required IRB documentation and examples, visit this page: Requirements for IRB Review and HIPAA Waiver Documentation for RIF DUA Request Submissions.
  • When is this documentation submitted (e.g., Before submitting a Data Use Agreement or accessing data in ResDAC?)
    • The investigator will submit a draft of the DUA research request packet to ResDAC for review and feedback.
    • While you can submit your draft request without IRB approval, however, before ResDAC finalizes the request packet for submission to the CMS Privacy Board for review and approval, your research project must have IRB review and documentation of compliance with the Common Rule and HIPAA Privacy Rule.
  • What other documentation is needed to ensure compliance with ethical and privacy regulations (e.g., the HIPAA Privacy Rule)?
    • As mentioned above, CMS will require documentation of the IRB review results and waivers (if any) to ensure compliance with the Common Rule and HIPAA Privacy Rule.
    • Please review this website to see the required IRB documentation.

Other Questions

  • After completing the analysis in the VRDC, how do you export the data?
    • CCW provides a detailed guide about the requirements and process of downloading outputs, and the file transfer system allows for a certain number of export request per seven days on a rolling basis. The number of exports depends on the status of DUA. Researchers and Innovators are allowed to export three and six times per week, respectively, but additional downloads per week can be purchased. The total size of files cannot exceed 1 gigabyte per each export. Before exporting, any output cells containing less than 11 observations and any personal identifiers must be redacted.
  • What types of files can be exported from the VRDC?
    • Most common file types can be exported (e.g., csv (*.csv), Excel (*.xlsx, *.xls, *.xlsb), SAS data sets (*.sas7bdat), or text formats (*.doc, *.docx, *.rtf, *.pdf, *.lst, *.txt) *.stata, and *.tsv. Some files types such as *.dat, *.do, *.dta, *.fts, *.gph, *.htm, *.html, *.jpeg, *.png, *.pptx, *.pqt, *.tab, and *.zip might increase the output review time.
  • Is there specific analytic software or skills you must have to analyze data in the VRDC?
    • VRDC provides different analytical software. The only software to initially access CMS data is SAS. After accessing the data with SAS you save the data and then use Python, R, or Stata to perform empirical analysis. To use Python, R, or Stata you need either an analytic container or data bricks. Our workflow is first to create the analysis data set in SAS and then use Python, R, or Stata depending on the team member.
  • Are there rules and/or guidelines investigators must follow to access the VRDC?
    • Investigators must follow strict rules when accessing the data through VRDC. Researcher must be physically present in the U.S. or U.S. territories, meet CCW security requirements (annual security training and HIPAA compliance), and comply with CCW data output review process.
  • Are there any states or years in the MAX data that do not have claims?
    • Yes, for various reasons some states were unable to submit Medicaid data for 2009 to 2011 and so are not in the MAX files. Specifically, the following files: MAX2009.MAXDATA_ME_OT (reference for Maine claims in 2009), MAX2010.MAXDATA_KS_OT, MAX2010.MAXDATA_KS_RX, MAX2010.MAXDATA_ME_OT, MAX2010.MAXDATA_ME_RX (reference for Maine and Kansas claims in 2010), and MAX2011.MAXDATA_ID_OT (paper reference skipping Idaho in 2011) are not available. Individuals from those states may or may not be missing from the eligibility, inpatient, and long term care files as well.
  • I accidentally locked myself out of the VRDC by typing my password incorrectly, what should I do?
    • There is an automated lockout system available at the CCW Login screen by clicking Forgot Password / Unlock account (or this link), typing in your email address, and then waiting approximately 15 minutes.

Tips for troubleshooting (Problems you may encounter)

Here are some common errors we encountered:

  • Unable to insert into temporary index while processing summary functions
    • This error appears in SAS EG when you perform an operation that requires too much memory. We encountered this error when we wanted to count the number of unique beneficiaries by some groups (e.g., age, race, sex, state) in a big file (~500 million rows) using proc sql. As an alternative you can first use proc sort nodupkey on the unique identifier and group variable and then proc freq on the group variable to obtain the count.
  • Unable to see any SAS claims files on the VRDC
    • This happens when there is a SAS error or other issue that causes you to be logged out of the SAS instance or your Cloud SAS profile. If that happens, click the bottom right 'No profile selected' button (it replaces 'Cloud SAS') and type in your credentials again under the profiles.
